01 Foundations

The Forex Trader's Guide to Major Economies

How central bank policy, GDP, trade flows, and economic data move currency pairs.

Every currency profile in this module follows the same structure, applying frameworks built across the entire curriculum: each country's central bank and its policy stance (Module 05), the data releases that matter most for that specific economy (Module 06), its structural trade relationships (Modules 14–15), and the practical implications for trading its currency pairs.

How to use this module: rather than memorizing every detail, focus on what makes each economy structurally distinct from the others — that's what actually helps you anticipate how each currency is likely to react to a shared global event differently.

Q Check Your Understanding

Quiz: Country Profiles

Click an answer to reveal the explanation.

1. Which central bank has the dual mandate of maximum employment and price stability?

Answer: B. The Federal Reserve operates under a dual mandate covering both employment and price stability.

2. Why has JPY historically served as a primary carry trade funding currency?

Answer: A. The BoJ's long-standing ultra-accommodative policy made JPY one of the cheapest currencies to borrow in, hence its frequent use as a funding currency.

3. Why does Chinese economic data often move AUD, NZD, and CAD as well as CNY itself?

Answer: A. As a major commodities consumer, China's growth outlook directly affects the export-driven economies of Australia, New Zealand, and Canada.

4. What distinguishes CNH from CNY?

Answer: A. China operates a dual exchange rate system, with the offshore CNH trading more freely than the more tightly managed onshore CNY.

5. Why does CHF often move on global risk sentiment headlines as much as on domestic Swiss data?

Answer: A. CHF's safe-haven role, rooted in political neutrality and a current account surplus, means global risk sentiment is a major independent driver alongside domestic data.
