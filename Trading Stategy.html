Your Trading System: The 4-Timeframe Top-Down Method

This is the exact system I want you to run before you ever click buy or sell — Daily, 4H, 1H, and 15M working together, in order, every single time. Nothing here is new theory. It's the price action, zone, confluence, multi-timeframe, and risk frameworks from this entire curriculum, assembled into one repeatable routine you can follow like a checklist.

00 Orientation

How This System Works

One job per timeframe. Never skip a step. Never work bottom-up.

Every timeframe in this system has exactly one job. You move through them in strict order — Daily first, then 4H, then 1H, then 15M — and you never let a "good-looking" 15M candle pull you into a trade before the higher timeframes have approved the idea. This is the top-down approach from Module 04, applied as a fixed daily habit.

The rule that protects you from yourself: if the Daily chart doesn't give you a clear bias, you don't trade that pair today. No exceptions. A confused higher timeframe means a confused trade, no matter how clean the 15M setup looks.
01 Timing

Your Daily Routine in Uganda Time (EAT)

Know exactly when to do each step of your analysis.

Uganda runs on East Africa Time (EAT), UTC+3. Here's how the four major forex sessions line up on your clock (times shift by about an hour depending on UK/US daylight saving — treat these as your standard reference):

SessionEAT (Uganda time)Character

Tokyo03:00 – 12:00Moderate volume, JPY pairs most active
London11:00 – 20:00High volume, the session that usually starts the day's real move
New York16:00 – 01:00High volume, often extends or reverses London's move

Your Recommended Daily Schedule

10:30 – 11:00Pre-London prep. Refine your 1H zone (Step 3). Set price alerts at your zone.
11:00 – 16:00London session live. Watch for setups forming on the 1H/15M as volume builds.

Why this matters: the exact same setup that works beautifully during the London/New York overlap can fail in quiet, low-liquidity hours simply because there isn't enough participation to drive follow-through. Timing isn't a footnote — it's part of the setup.
02 Step 1

The Daily Chart: Establishing Your Bias

Your job here is singular: decide whether this pair is trending up, trending down, or ranging.

What To Look For

  • Market structure (Module 01): label the last 5–8 swing highs and lows. Higher Highs + Higher Lows = uptrend. Lower Highs + Lower Lows = downtrend. Roughly equal highs/lows = range.
  • Major supply/demand zones (Module 01): mark the two or three most significant zones — the origin of sharp, impulsive moves — currently visible on the chart.
  • Trend strength check (Module 03): glance at ADX and whether price is "walking the band" on Bollinger Bands to confirm whether this is genuinely trending or quietly rotating in a range.
  • Recent structural break check (Module 01): has there been a recent Change of Character (CHoCH)? That's your earliest clue the bias might be shifting.
Your Daily output, written down: "[Pair] is in a [Daily uptrend / downtrend / range]. The nearest [demand / supply] zone in my favored direction sits at [price]." That single sentence is what you carry into Step 2.
03 Step 2

The 4H Chart: Zooming Into the Zone

Your job: confirm the Daily zone still looks valid, and tighten its boundaries.

What To Look For

  • Confirm the Daily zone hasn't already been used up. Check how many times it's been retested (Module 01) — a fresh zone is worth more than one tested repeatedly.
  • Refine the zone's exact boundaries. The Daily chart gives you the general area; the 4H lets you tighten it to the actual base candles of the original impulsive move.
  • Check 4H structure agrees with the Daily bias. If Daily says uptrend but 4H structure shows a developing CHoCH, slow down — this is a genuine disagreement worth waiting out.
  • Note any 4H trendline or channel boundary (Grade 1) intersecting the same zone — early confluence building.
A genuine disagreement between Daily and 4H is information, not noise. If structure conflicts, the correct response is patience — wait for clarity rather than forcing the next steps anyway.

05 Step 4

The 15M Chart: Your Entry Trigger

Your job: wait for one specific candle to confirm, right at your 1H zone — then act.

Remember the rule from Module 01, lesson 5: a candlestick pattern only matters because of where it forms. On the 15M chart, you are only watching for these patterns when price is actually trading inside the 1H confluence zone from Step 3 — the same pattern anywhere else on the chart is noise.

The Three Trigger Patterns to Watch

PatternWhat confirms itEntry point
Pin bar / rejection wickClose of the pin bar, back inside the prior rangeOn the close, or a break of the pin bar's high (for longs)
Engulfing candleA full-bodied candle closing beyond the entire prior candle's rangeOn the close of the engulfing candle
Inside bar breakoutA clean break of the inside bar's high or low, in the favored directionOn the break itself, or a retest of the inside bar's edge
If 30–45 minutes pass at your zone with no qualifying candle, stand aside. Chasing an entry that never confirmed is how a well-analyzed trade idea turns into an undisciplined loss.
06 Risk & Targets

Stop Loss, Position Sizing & Target

The mechanical part — never skipped, never improvised.

Stop Loss Placement (Module 27)

Place your stop just beyond the far edge of your 1H zone, plus a small ATR buffer to avoid common liquidity-grab spikes — never at the zone's exact edge, and never based on "how much you're willing to lose."

Position Size (Module 26)

Risk 0.5–1% of your account per trade (Module 24) — tighter while you're still validating this system against your own results.

Target (Modules 01, 02, 24)

  • Aim first for the next opposing structural level on the 1H or 4H chart — a prior swing high/low or the opposite edge of a range.
  • Confirm your reward-to-risk ratio is at least 1.5:1, ideally 2:1 or better, before entering.
  • Consider scaling out a portion at the first target and trailing the remainder (Module 28) if the higher-timeframe trend remains intact.
07 Before You Click Buy or Sell

The Full Pre-Trade Checklist

Run through this, every time, with no exceptions.

  • Daily chart bias is clear: trending up, trending down, or genuinely ranging — written down in one sentence.
  • 4H zone confirmed, refined to its precise boundaries, and checked for freshness.
  • 1H confluence checklist scores at least 3 of 6 factors.
  • Price is currently trading inside the qualified 1H zone — not approaching it, not far from it.
  • A valid 15M trigger candle (pin bar, engulfing, or inside bar breakout) has actually closed and confirmed.
  • Stop loss is placed just beyond the zone, with an ATR buffer — calculated, not guessed.
  • Position size is calculated from account size, risk %, and stop distance — not a habitual lot size.
  • Reward-to-risk ratio to the next structural target is at least 1.5:1.
  • The current time is within or approaching the London/New York overlap (16:00–20:00 EAT) — or you have a specific reason to act outside it.
  • No major scheduled news release for this pair is due in the next 30 minutes (Module 11).
